The Legend of Zelda NES remake:

I worked on Link's mechanics (movement, attack, weapons), the level design/enemy placement, as well as a few enemies (wallmaster, bladetraps). I also worked on the custom level design and dash mechanic, and a portion of the audio and visuals. The project was done using Unity in C#.
The Windows and MacOS builds are available here: The Legend of Zelda NES remake

Orbital Mechanics:

I made an original puzzle platforming physics game that uses real orbital mechanics in a fun way. If you want to gain an intuitive understanding of orbital mechanics that even the Gemini 4 spaceflight team hadn't figured out, play this game! The project was done using Unity in C#.
The Windows and MacOS builds are available here: Orbital Mechanics

Kai: Lost Waters

I helped make an original 2d puzzle platformer with dynamic water. I made the physics/mechanics, visuals and audio of

  • the water and its interactions including water spawners, vaporizing to steam, evaporating, glowing, etc
  • the water abilities including the bending (left click) and blast (right click)
  • the stone sliding doors/platforms, attached wires, and droplet "buttons"
  • the springy capsule-shaped glowing platforms
  • the raising water platforms
with the exception of the sprites for the sliding door and capsule-shaped platforms, and half the sprite of the raising platforms.

Other contributions include:
  • All the audio was also done by me with the exception of the menu click sounds.
  • I also did the visuals (flame) and audio of the gecko enemies (and the death), with the exception of the sprite and its animation
  • I was a core level and puzzle designer, and contributed to other gameplay mechanics including the thorns and logs
  • The starting and ending cutscenes were also strung together by me, and I drew the first shot of the ending cutscene.

The project was done using Unity in C#.
The Windows and MacOS builds are available here: Kai: Lost Waters
